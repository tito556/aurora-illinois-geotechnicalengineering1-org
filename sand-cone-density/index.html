Common questions

How much does a field density test with the sand cone cost in Aurora?

A single sand cone test typically runs between US$100 and US$130, depending on site access and the number of tests per mobilization. Volume discounts apply when you schedule a full day of testing with multiple technicians.

How long does one sand cone test take on site?

About 20 to 30 minutes per point from excavation to weight recording. If the material needs a moisture content determination by oven drying, add 24 hours for the lab result, though we can provide a field-speedy estimate with a hotplate for immediate go/no-go decisions.

What's the advantage of the sand cone over a nuclear gauge?

The sand cone is a direct measurement of volume and mass—it doesn't need calibration against a specific soil's chemistry or moisture. In Aurora's glacial clays with variable iron content, the sand cone eliminates the chemical bias that can shift nuclear gauge readings by several percent.

When does IDOT require sand cone testing instead of a nuclear gauge?

IDOT specs often require sand cone verification when nuclear gauge results are in dispute or when the material contains high organic content, slag, or other atypical constituents. The sand cone also serves as the referee method for calibration checks on nuclear gauges per ASTM D6938.
